lazy-hsa

Pay medical bills now. Invest your HSA. Reimburse decades later.

pip install lazy-hsa

A local AI-powered CLI that scans receipts, extracts data with vision models, and tracks everything in Google Sheets — so you can claim HSA reimbursements years from now when your investments have compounded.

How It Works

1

Drop receipts

Upload medical bills, EOBs, or pharmacy receipts to your Google Drive inbox folder.

2

AI extracts

Local vision models (Ollama) read each document and pull out dates, amounts, and providers.

3

Sheets tracks

Everything lands in a Google Sheets master index — organized by year, patient, and category.

Key Features

  • 100% local AI — Your medical data never leaves your machine. Uses Ollama with vision models for extraction.
  • Provider-specific skills — Custom extraction logic for 10+ providers (Aetna, CVS, Costco, Express Scripts, Sutter, and more).
  • Multi-claim EOBs — Handles insurance EOBs with multiple patients, dates, and claim lines in a single document.
  • Reconciliation & overbilling detection — Matches EOBs to statements, flags when you were charged more than insurance says you owe.
  • Reimbursement view — See exactly which visits are ready to claim, what's pending, and total claimable amount.
  • Gmail scanning — Automatically finds medical emails with attachments and processes them.

Quick Start

# Install
pip install lazy-hsa

# Set up Google API credentials and local AI
lazy-hsa setup

# Process files from your Drive inbox
lazy-hsa inbox

# Or process a single file
lazy-hsa process --file receipt.pdf

# View reconciliation and reimbursement summary
lazy-hsa reconcile --year 2025

See the full setup guide for Google API configuration and Ollama installation.

The Strategy

The Boglehead HSA strategy: pay medical expenses out-of-pocket today, let your HSA investments grow tax-free for decades, then reimburse yourself later. The IRS has no time limit on HSA reimbursements — you just need to keep your receipts.

lazy-hsa is the "keep your receipts" part, automated.
